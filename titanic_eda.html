Key Findings & Conclusions
01

Gender was the strongest predictor of survival

Females had a 74% survival rate vs 18.9% for males — the single most impactful feature in the dataset, clearly reflecting the "women and children first" maritime protocol.

02

Socioeconomic class created a survival hierarchy

1st class (62.9%) vs 3rd class (24.2%) survival rates show that wealth significantly influenced access to lifeboats, either through cabin location or priority access.

03

Children had relatively higher survival rates

The 0–10 age group showed elevated survival across both genders, consistent with evacuation protocols prioritizing children alongside women.

04

Fare and Class are highly collinear predictors

Fare (r=+0.257) and Pclass (r=−0.338) both correlate with survival but carry overlapping information — for modeling, one may be dropped to avoid multicollinearity.

05

Missing age data (19.9%) requires careful imputation

Using median age per class/gender combination for imputation is more accurate than overall median imputation — an important preprocessing step before ML modeling.
